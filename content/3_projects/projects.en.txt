  project_categories: Graduates 2021

  project_description_left: |
    It's almost here – just a few weeks until the premiere of this year's graduation project! In two programs, each featuring different graduating classes and choreographers, you can immerse yourselves in the works based on this year's theme, "On the Trail," and be inspired!
    
    Each year, the graduation project, the final examination performance of our graduates, takes place under a different theme. Raul Valdez, artistic director of CDSH – Contemporary Dance School Hamburg, draws inspiration for the theme from the energy and dynamism of each graduating class. "On the Trail" symbolizes the essence of the work of the 2021 graduating class, which crystallized during their three years of training, and serves as both a guiding theme and a space for experimentation for all the choreographers.
    
    "On the Trail" describes the situation after a turning point, the moment when things come together again after being shaken or even broken. In this moment of apparent chaos, where normality no longer functions or only functions to a limited extent, lies the potential to sense the essence of things and redefine normality.
  project_description_right: |
    Structures develop, habits become ingrained. What is perceived as normal is the result of a constant, sometimes unreflective, reproduction of what is considered right, desirable, and productive. We function and forget to question.
    
    When a system is shaken, the possibility arises to look into the voids between the shards, to consider the other side of our reality, to perceive individual aspects that were not visible in the functioning system.
    
    What does it mean to continue after such a shock, in and after a turbulent time? What can we learn? How do we rethink and adapt to new circumstances, how do we rebel against the old, how do we give a voice to the forgotten? In any case, the result is a transformation, a new awareness, and the beginning of a new path.
    
    This year, our year-end performances can also take place live in the theatre – even with a small audience (50 spectators per performance)!

  project_id: meeting_point
  project_name: Final projet
  project_title: Meeting Point
  header_image:
    - file://illdyjnv0svddgil
  project_categories: Graduates 2025
  project_subjects: Choreography
  project_description_left: |
    Each year, our Work in Progress and graduation performances take place under a different theme. Raul Valdez, artistic director of CDSH – Contemporary Dance School Hamburg, draws inspiration for the theme from the energy and dynamism of the respective graduating class. This year, “meeting point” is both the overarching theme and a space for experimentation for all choreographers and dancers.
    
    The “Work in Progress” performances offer a glimpse into the work of the upcoming graduation project in summer 2025.
    
    A “meeting point” is primarily a specific place in time and space. In antiquity, it was a place in the centers of city-states where people with diverse, often opposing, opinions and perspectives could come together to discuss and shape political reality. A meeting point that marks the birthplace of modern democracy.
    
    When people come together, they do so not only from different geographical directions, but also with different social backgrounds, education, cultures, and religious and political convictions. A meeting place is therefore not just a place to arrive, but also a place of departure: Here, people share their diverse perspectives and ideals and leave with new outlooks.
  project_description_right: |
    The openness to see and hear one another requires an awareness of the complexity of reality, of contradictions, gray areas, and overlaps. True solutions do not lie in crude oversimplifications, rigid ideologies, and the demonization of opposing views, but in the nuances that lie between stereotypes and extremes.
    
    In times when fronts are hardening, populist rhetoric is mobilizing people, and political realities are being shaped, coming together is of particular importance. The world is not made up of a series of absolutes, but of a web of different truths. The only way to reconcile them is to listen to one another and to meet each other with empathy.
    
    Last but not least, we also find such meeting points within ourselves, when different aspects of the self enter into a dialogue: our own past and present, different emotional states, or contradictory desires and values.
